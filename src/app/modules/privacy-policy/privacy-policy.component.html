Cookies Policy

This Cookies Policy explains how VTES Decks uses cookies and similar technologies to recognize you when you visit https://vtesdecks.com (the "Websites"), what they are, and how you can control them.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small data files placed on your device when you visit a website. "First party cookies" are set by the website you visit; "third party cookies" are set by other parties to provide features such as advertising and analytics.

Why do we use cookies?

Some cookies are strictly necessary for the Websites to work. Others — which we only set with your consent — help us measure usage and show advertising. The specific technologies we use are described below.

Essential cookies and storage

These are strictly necessary to provide the Websites and their features, such as keeping you signed in, remembering your language and consent choices, and protecting against abuse. Because they are essential, they are not subject to consent, but you can block them in your browser (in which case parts of the Websites may not work).

Provider Purpose
VTES Decks (vtesdecks.com) Authentication, preferences, and consent choices.
Google reCAPTCHA (google.com) Protecting forms and the Websites against abuse and bots.
Cloudflare (cloudflare.com) Security, content delivery, and cookieless traffic analytics.

Analytics cookies

These help us understand how the Websites are used. They are set only with your consent.

Provider Opt-out / more info
Google Analytics (google.com) https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

Advertising cookies

These are used to deliver and measure advertising, and in some cases to show ads based on your interests. They are set only with your consent, and are not used for accounts with an active supporter contribution.

Provider Opt-out / more info
Google AdSense (ads.google.com) https://www.google.com/settings/ads

Other tracking technologies

We may also use pixels or web beacons — tiny files that let us recognize when someone has visited our Websites or opened an email, helping us measure usage and the effectiveness of communications. These often rely on cookies, so declining cookies will limit their function.

How can I control cookies?

You can accept or reject non-essential cookies at any time using the cookie-settings control on the Websites. You can also set your browser to refuse or delete cookies; if you do, some features may not work. Because browser controls differ, please check your browser's help pages for instructions.

We may update this Cookies Policy to reflect changes to the technologies we use or for legal or operational reasons. The "Last updated" date at the top of this document indicates when it was last revised.
